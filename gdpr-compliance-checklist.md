## 1. Lawful Basis for Processing

- [ ] All personal data processing activities have a documented lawful basis
- [ ] Consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous where used
- [ ] Consent records are maintained with date, method, and scope
- [ ] Users can withdraw consent at any time and the process is easy
- [ ] Processing without consent is documented with the applicable legal basis
- [ ] Legitimate interests assessment conducted where legitimate interests is the lawful basis

---

## 2. Privacy Policy

- [ ] Privacy policy is published and easily accessible
- [ ] Privacy policy is written in clear and plain language
- [ ] Privacy policy states what data is collected
- [ ] Privacy policy states the lawful basis for each processing activity
- [ ] Privacy policy states how long data is retained
- [ ] Privacy policy states who data is shared with
- [ ] Privacy policy includes details of international transfers
- [ ] Privacy policy explains data subject rights
- [ ] Privacy policy includes contact details for the Data Protection Officer or privacy contact
- [ ] Privacy policy is reviewed and updated at least annually

---

## 3. Data Subject Rights

- [ ] Process in place for handling subject access requests (SARs)
- [ ] Process in place for handling right to erasure requests
- [ ] Process in place for handling right to rectification requests
- [ ] Process in place for handling right to data portability requests
- [ ] Process in place for handling right to restrict processing requests
- [ ] Process in place for handling right to object requests
- [ ] Response to data subject requests completed within one month
- [ ] Data subject rights process communicated to users in privacy policy

---

## 4. Data Protection Officer

- [ ] DPO appointed where required (public authority, large scale processing, or sensitive data)
- [ ] DPO contact details published in privacy policy
- [ ] DPO registered with supervisory authority where required
- [ ] DPO has appropriate knowledge of data protection law
- [ ] DPO reports directly to senior management
- [ ] DPO role and responsibilities documented

---

## 5. Records of Processing Activities

- [ ] Record of processing activities (ROPA) maintained
- [ ] ROPA includes name and contact details of controller
- [ ] ROPA includes purposes of processing
- [ ] ROPA includes categories of data subjects and personal data
- [ ] ROPA includes categories of recipients
- [ ] ROPA includes details of international transfers
- [ ] ROPA includes retention periods
- [ ] ROPA reviewed and updated regularly

---

## 6. Data Security

- [ ] Personal data encrypted at rest
- [ ] Personal data encrypted in transit
- [ ] Access to personal data restricted to authorised staff
- [ ] Access controls and authentication enforced
- [ ] Security measures reviewed at least annually
- [ ] Staff trained on data protection obligations
- [ ] Vulnerability assessments conducted regularly

---

## 7. Data Breach Management

- [ ] Data breach response procedure documented
- [ ] Process to notify supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware
- [ ] Process to notify affected individuals without undue delay where required
- [ ] Breach register maintained
- [ ] All breaches and near misses logged and assessed

---

## 8. Data Retention

- [ ] Data retention policy documented
- [ ] Retention periods defined for each category of personal data
- [ ] Process in place for secure deletion at end of retention period
- [ ] Retention periods communicated in privacy policy

---

## 9. Third Party Processors

- [ ] All third party processors identified and documented
- [ ] Due diligence conducted on all processors before engagement
- [ ] Data Processing Agreements signed with all processors
- [ ] Processor compliance reviewed at least annually
- [ ] Sub-processor arrangements reviewed and approved

---

## 10. International Data Transfers

- [ ] All transfers of personal data outside the EEA identified and documented
- [ ] Appropriate safeguards in place for each transfer (adequacy decision, SCCs, BCRs)
- [ ] Transfer impact assessments conducted where required
- [ ] International transfer details included in privacy policy

---

## 11. Data Protection Impact Assessments

- [ ] DPIA process documented
- [ ] DPIAs conducted for high risk processing activities
- [ ] DPIAs reviewed and updated when processing changes
- [ ] Supervisory authority consulted where DPIA identifies high residual risk

---

## 12. Children's Data

- [ ] Age verification in place where services are directed at children
- [ ] Parental consent obtained for children under 16 where required
- [ ] Privacy information presented in child-friendly format where applicable
