## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the process for handling data subject rights requests under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). It ensures that all requests are handled consistently, promptly, and in compliance with GDPR requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all personal data held by the organisation and all staff who handle data subject requests.

---

## 3. Data Subject Rights Under GDPR

| Right | Description | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Right of Access | Request a copy of personal data held | 1 month |
| Right to Rectification | Request correction of inaccurate data | 1 month |
| Right to Erasure | Request deletion of personal data | 1 month |
| Right to Restrict Processing | Request restriction of processing | 1 month |
| Right to Data Portability | Request data in portable format | 1 month |
| Right to Object | Object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing | 1 month |
| Rights re Automated Decisions | Request human review of automated decisions | 1 month |

## 5. Identity Verification

Before processing any request the organisation must verify the identity of the data subject to prevent unauthorised disclosure.

Accepted verification methods:
- Confirmation of account email and registered details
- Government issued ID for sensitive or high risk requests
- Security questions where applicable

For requests submitted on behalf of another person, written authorisation from the data subject must be provided.

---

## 6. Response Timeframes

| Stage | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Acknowledgement | Within 3 business days |
| Full response | Within 1 month of receipt |
| Complex requests | Up to 3 months with notification to data subject within 1 month |

Where the organisation cannot action a request within one month, the data subject must be notified of the extension and the reason before the end of the first month.

## 7. Handling Each Request Type

### 7.1 Right of Access (Subject Access Request)

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Locate all personal data held for that individual across all systems
3. Compile a Subject Access Response including all personal data held
4. Deliver the response securely within one month
5. Include information on the purposes of processing, retention periods, and data subject rights
6. Log the request and response in the request register

### 7.2 Right to Rectification

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Review the inaccurate or incomplete data identified
3. Correct the data across all systems
4. Notify any third parties who received the inaccurate data
5. Confirm rectification to the data subject in writing

### 7.3 Right to Erasure

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Assess whether erasure is legally required or if an exemption applies
3. If required, delete data from all systems including backups where feasible
4. Notify third party processors to erase the data
5. Confirm erasure to the data subject in writing
6. Document any data retained and the legal basis for retention

**Exemptions where erasure may not apply:**
- Legal obligation to retain the data
- Public interest or scientific research purposes
- Establishment or defence of legal claims

### 7.4 Right to Restrict Processing

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Assess the grounds for restriction
3. Apply a restriction flag to the data in all relevant systems
4. Continue to store but not process the data
5. Notify the data subject before lifting any restriction

### 7.5 Right to Data Portability

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Compile the data provided by the data subject in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (CSV or JSON)
3. Deliver securely to the data subject or directly to another controller if requested
4. Complete within one month

### 7.6 Right to Object

1. Assess the grounds for objection
2. For direct marketing objections — stop processing immediately, no exceptions
3. For legitimate interests objections — assess whether compelling legitimate grounds override the objection
4. Respond to the data subject within one month with the outcome and reasoning

### 7.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision Making

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Arrange for a human review of the automated decision
3. Provide the data subject with an explanation of the decision
4. Allow the data subject to contest the decision
5. Respond within one month

---

## 8. Requests That May Be Refused

The organisation may refuse a request where:

- The request is manifestly unfounded or excessive
- Fulfilling the request would adversely affect the rights of others
- A legal exemption applies

All refusals must be documented with clear reasoning and communicated to the data subject within one month. The data subject must also be informed of their right to complain to the supervisory authority.

---

## 9. Fees

Data subject requests must be responded to free of charge. A reasonable fee may be charged where requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive, but this must be justified and documented.

## 11. Staff Responsibilities

All staff who receive a data subject request must:

- Forward it to the Data Protection Officer or privacy contact immediately
- Not attempt to handle the request without authorisation
- Not discuss the request with unauthorised parties

---

## 12. Supervisory Authority Complaints

If a data subject is not satisfied with the organisation's response they have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant supervisory authority. Staff must inform data subjects of this right in all responses.
