## 1. Purpose

A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) is required under Article 35 of the GDPR where processing is likely to result in a high risk to individuals. This template guides organisations through the DPIA process to identify, assess, and mitigate data protection risks before processing begins.

---

## 2. When is a DPIA Required?

A DPIA is mandatory where processing is likely to result in high risk, particularly when:

- [ ] Systematic and extensive profiling or automated decision making with significant effects
- [ ] Large scale processing of special category data or criminal conviction data
- [ ] Systematic monitoring of a publicly accessible area on a large scale
- [ ] New technologies being used
- [ ] Processing that prevents individuals from exercising their rights or accessing services
- [ ] Large scale processing of personal data relating to children
- [ ] Processing involving biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying individuals

## 6. Risk Scoring Guide

| Score | Likelihood | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Very unlikely | Negligible impact on individuals |
| 2 | Unlikely | Minor impact, individuals can overcome |
| 3 | Possible | Significant impact requiring effort to overcome |
| 4 | Likely | Serious impact affecting daily life |
| 5 | Very likely | Severe or irreversible impact |

**Risk Score = Likelihood x Severity**

| Risk Score | Risk Level | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1-4 | Low | Accept, document, and monitor |
| 5-9 | Medium | Implement additional controls |
| 10-16 | High | Must be mitigated before processing begins |
| 17-25 | Critical | Consider whether processing should proceed, consult supervisory authority |
